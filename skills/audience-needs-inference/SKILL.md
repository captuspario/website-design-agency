# Audience Needs Inference

## Purpose

Create a lightweight, decision-useful view of what likely visitors need from the website without pretending full user research exists.

## Inputs

- Business context summary
- Existing website
- Competitor notes
- Available enquiry or sales signals

## Outputs

- Audience group summary
- Key needs and objections
- Decision criteria notes
- Confidence and assumption markers

## Steps

1. Identify likely audience segments from the service model.
2. Infer top questions, anxieties, and decision triggers.
3. Capture what proof or clarity each segment likely needs.
4. Note which inferences are strong and which are weak.

## Done criteria

- Audience outputs are short and usable by design and content roles.
- Assumptions are marked clearly.
- Insights connect directly to website decisions.

## Escalation

Escalate when the project needs real user evidence that cannot be inferred responsibly from available inputs.
