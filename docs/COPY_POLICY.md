# Copy Policy

## Core rules

- Use plain language.
- Do not invent claims.
- Do not use manipulative, cheesy, or inflated wording.
- Preserve useful specifics from the client where they increase credibility.
- Improve clarity, structure, and scanability.

## Confirmation discipline

- Treat business facts, credentials, service details, and proof claims as confirmed only when supported.
- Mark inference versus confirmed fact where the distinction matters to decisions.

## Editing priorities

- Tighten headings and subheadings.
- Remove vague filler.
- Clarify service scope and next steps.
- Keep CTA language direct and honest.

## Escalation

Escalate when the copy needs facts, proof, legal review, or client sign-off that is not yet available.
