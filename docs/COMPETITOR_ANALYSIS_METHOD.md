# Competitor Analysis Method

## Purpose

Benchmark direct and adjacent competitors in a way that improves decisions about trust, positioning, structure, and website quality.

## Focus areas

- Information architecture
- Navigation patterns
- Message hierarchy
- Proof and trust cues
- Offer framing
- Service-page conventions
- Visual tone and common clichés

## Method

1. Choose a tight set of relevant competitors.
2. Review homepage, service pages, contact paths, and trust sections.
3. Record repeated category patterns and weak generic habits.
4. Identify opportunities to be clearer, more credible, or easier to use.
5. Convert findings into practical direction for content, IA, UX, and UI.

## Output standard

- What the category usually does
- What appears overused or weak
- What seems to support trust
- Where the client can differentiate without gimmicks

## Hard limits

- Do not copy competitor structures blindly.
- Do not overfit to one competitor.
- Keep the analysis usable for real project decisions.
