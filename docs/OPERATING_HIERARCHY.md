# Operating Hierarchy

Use this hierarchy to keep the company acting through management lines rather than pulling the `ceo` into specialist work.

## Executive chain

The default management chain is:

1. `ceo`
2. `project-manager` and `creative-director`
3. team leads
4. specialist contributors

## Leadership team

- `ceo`: final scope, sequencing, and approval owner
- `project-manager`: operations lead and workflow controller
- `creative-director`: design-quality lead

The `ceo` should work through this leadership layer first. It should not bypass the chain to perform competitor analysis, audience inference, or other specialist work directly.

## Team leads

- Strategy and Research lead: `business-context-analyst`
- Experience Design lead: `product-designer`
- Production lead: `wordpress-builder`
- Quality lead: `technical-qa-reviewer`
- Reporting lead: `report-writer`

## Specialist contributors

### Strategy and Research

- `market-competitor-analyst`
- `ux-researcher`
- `content-messaging-strategist`

### Experience Design

- `information-architect`
- `ux-designer`
- `ui-designer`

### Production

- `seo-analyst`
- `analytics-checker`

### Quality

- `ux-qa-reviewer`
- `accessibility-qa-reviewer`
- `handover-reviewer`

## Delegation rules

- `ceo` delegates through leadership.
- `project-manager` delegates through team leads where a team lead exists.
- `creative-director` works through `product-designer` for execution and synthesis inside experience design.
- team leads delegate to their specialist contributors.
- specialists do not bypass their lead unless escalation is required.

## Approval rules

- strategy and execution should continue without board-operator involvement until a real approval gate is reached
- when an approval gate is reached, the `ceo` presents the package and recommendation
- when a workflow stall happens, the `ceo` restores momentum through leadership and team leads instead of taking over the specialist task directly

## Anti-patterns

These are signs the system is slipping:

- `ceo` performing competitor analysis or audience inference itself
- `project-manager` doing specialist work instead of routing it
- specialists routing directly to the `ceo` when a team lead should synthesize first
- QA fixing strategy issues without returning them upstream
- production redefining design or content direction without escalation
